Quick Setup

Get Started in Under 2 Minutes.

Follow these quick steps to set up Textora in developer mode and start capturing text in seconds.

1. Prepare & Install

  • 1
    Download the ZIP

    Download the extension ZIP archive to your computer.

  • 2
    Extract the Archive

    Extract (unzip) the downloaded textora-extension.zip file into a normal folder of your choice.

  • 3
    Enable Developer Mode

    Open Google Chrome, navigate to chrome://extensions/, and toggle Developer mode in the top-right corner.

  • 4
    Load Unpacked Folder

    Click the Load unpacked button (top-left) and select the extracted folder containing the manifest.json file.

2. How to Use

  • 1
    Pin for Easy Access

    Click the Extensions puzzle icon in the Chrome toolbar and click the pin icon next to Textora.

  • 2
    Activate a Selection Mode

    Click the Textora icon to open the popup, or use hotkeys: Alt+S (Area), Alt+I (OCR), or Alt+L (Links).

  • 3
    Extract & Auto-Copy

    Select your content. Extracted text is instantly copied to your clipboard and logged in your secure history drawer.

Architecture

Built for Speed & Privacy.

Textora runs natively within your Chrome sandbox, keeping your browser fast and your private data completely secure.

100% Privacy Focused

We do not store or transmit your extracted text. Offline Tesseract.js processing is done entirely on your machine. Gemini OCR runs directly through Chrome's secure network layer.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions.

Do I need a Gemini API Key to use this?

No, you do not. Textora includes Tesseract.js running fully locally in your browser to perform OCR offline. If you want maximum speed and accuracy for complex text, you can configure your own Gemini API Key (Gemini 2.5 Flash is currently free to use).

How does "Selected Area" differ from standard Chrome text selection?

Standard selection forces you to select lines of text sequentially. Selected Area lets you draw a physical bounding box. It then traverses the page's DOM elements and extracts only the text that visually overlaps with that box. Ideal for columns, graphs, and tables.

Is my clipboard history shared or backed up?

Never. Your history is stored inside the local storage of your web browser (`chrome.storage.local`). We have no servers, no analytic scripts tracking your copies, and zero access to your captured text.

What are the shortcut keys?

You can toggle actions instantly using the standard hotkeys: Alt+S (Selected Area), Alt+I (Copy from Image OCR), and Alt+L (Copy Link Text). These shortcuts can be customized in `chrome://extensions/shortcuts`.
